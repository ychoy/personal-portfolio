About

Bold, collaborative and dedicated, I am a full stack web developer with a knack for seamless design, user and impact-driven development, and business acumen. My previous career as a nonprofit manager inspired me to pursue my true passion: building web applications. I was not satisfied with only being involved in product management—I wanted to build web applications. In the next phase of my career, I will use technology to create solutions that make an impact, improve workflow and and empower people to communicate better. I am now learning full stack web development, building full stack web applications using MEAN stack, such as CarpeGov, a RESTful open source civic engagement tool for users to CRUD Congressional bills and action items taken on bills.

I love to augment my programming skills with my strong communication, problem-solving, leadership, creative and analytic abilities. In my previous role, I worked with government clients to determine project scope and identify and manage volunteer developers to contribute to civic application projects such as a performance management data dashboard or a transportation mobile application. By facilitating thorough planning meetings, the user and developers had a clear idea of project deliverables, design and user experience.

In my free time, I volunteer with Code for San José on Find A Home, an open source full-stack affordable housing application. I also serve as the Co-Director of New Leaders Council Silicon Valley. I am looking for opportunities with startups or companies that employ best practices such as Test-Driven Development and pair programming to refine my programming skills as a full stack web developer.
